A short history

Cebu Province

Cebu is widely regarded as the birthplace of Christianity in the Philippines, marked by Ferdinand Magellan's 1521 arrival and the planting of Magellan's Cross. It later grew into the country's first Spanish settlement and capital, Villa San Miguel, under Miguel López de Legazpi in 1565. Centuries of trade turned Cebu City into the "Queen City of the South," a hub linking the central Visayas to the rest of the archipelago. Today the province blends that colonial-era heritage — centuries-old churches, forts, and plazas — with the rolling hills of Busay and Toledo, and the white-sand islands of Bantayan, Malapascua, and Camotes just offshore.

City Tour

7–9 stops · The Heritage & Hills Route
1
Where it began

Sto. Niño Church

The oldest Roman Catholic church in the Philippines, home to the revered Sto. Niño image — the heart of Cebu's faith.

2
First landmark

Magellan's Cross

Planted in 1521, this wooden cross marks the spot where Christianity was first introduced to the islands.

3
Oldest fort

Fort San Pedro

A triangular Spanish military fortress by the harbor, now a quiet garden-and-museum stop with centuries of history in its walls.

4
Quiet & red

Taoist Temple

A serene hillside temple with sweeping city views, dragon-carved stairways, and a place to make a quiet wish.

5
City from above

Tops Lookout

The highest viewpoint over Cebu City and the strait beyond — best for sunset and skyline photos.

6
A son's tribute

Temple of Leah

Cebu's own "Taj Mahal" — a grand Roman-inspired temple built as a monument to lasting love.

7
Last stop, golden hour

Sirao Garden

Famously known as the "Little Amsterdam of Cebu," a vibrant hillside flower farm in the mountains of Busay, Cebu City.

8
Optional add-on

Buwakan ni Alejandra

A colorful mountain garden with thousands of blooming flowers, scenic viewpoints, and relaxing nature walks.

9
Optional add-on

10,000 Roses

A flower-filled garden café in the hills — the prettiest place to end the day with a coffee and a view.

South Cebu Tour

Waterfalls, whale sharks & the southern coast
1
Miraculous shrine

Simala Church

A grand, castle-like shrine in Sibonga known for its towering monastery architecture and the pilgrims who visit seeking miracles.

2
Gentle giants

Oslob Whale Sharks

Swim alongside gentle whale sharks in the clear waters of Tan-awan, Oslob — a rare, up-close encounter with the world's largest fish.

3
Curtain of white

Tumalog Falls

A soft, wide waterfall in Samboan where fine mist curtains down over limestone rock — one of Cebu's most photogenic falls.

4
Turquoise & canyons

Kawasan Falls

Three-tiered turquoise falls in Badian, popular for canyoneering, cliff jumps, and bamboo raft rides through the canyon.

5
Underwater spectacle

Sardine Run

Snorkel or dive just meters from shore in Moalboal to swim through a massive, swirling school of sardines.
